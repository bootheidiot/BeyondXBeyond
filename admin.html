Finding Video Source URLs

BeyondXBeyond doesn't stream anything itself. You paste a player URL into the admin panel, and your viewers' browsers load that player directly — synced to your broadcast clock.

The trick is finding the raw player URL buried inside sites like AniCrush, rather than the page URL. Here's how.

Method: Browser DevTools (works on any streaming site)

1
Open the episode page on AniCrush (or gogoanime, zoro.to, etc.) and let the player load.
2
Press F12 to open DevTools. Click the Elements tab.
3
Press Ctrl+F (search in elements) and type iframe. Look for an iframe whose src points to a player domain (examples below). That src value is your embed URL.
4
Alternatively use the Network tab: reload the page, filter by Media or search for .m3u8 or .mp4. Right-click the request → Copy URL. These are the raw stream files.
5
Paste the URL into the admin panel's Embed URL field and set the correct duration.

YouTube (Most Reliable)

For classic shows, many full episodes are on YouTube. Just paste the regular video URL — BeyondXBeyond converts it automatically and seeks to the correct position.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XXXXXXXXXXX  ← paste as-is
https://youtu.be/XXXXXXXXXXX                 ← also works

Raw .m3u8 or .mp4 URLs

If you find a direct stream URL in the Network tab, BeyondXBeyond can play these using HLS.js (for .m3u8) or a native video element (for .mp4). Just paste them in — the player auto-detects the format.

https://cdn.example.com/episode1/index.m3u8   ← HLS stream
https://cdn.example.com/episode1/ep.mp4        ← Direct video

Direct stream URLs often expire after a few hours, so for scheduled broadcasts plan to update them day-of.

Sync Accuracy Notes

BeyondXBeyond syncs using UTC time. When a viewer tunes in, their browser calculates how many seconds into the current slot it is and seeks the video to that point. YouTube embeds support exact seeking via the ?start= parameter. Generic iframes receive a ?t= hint but may not respond to it depending on the player — in those cases there may be a few seconds of drift on late joins.

For best sync accuracy: use YouTube sources where possible, and set episode durations precisely (minutes + seconds).
